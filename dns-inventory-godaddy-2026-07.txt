DECISION — 2026-07-20 (Chris unavailable; worst-case default applied)
============================================================================
Principle: the nameserver flip must be BEHAVIOR-NEUTRAL for everything except the
website root + www. When we cannot confirm whether a record is load-bearing, we COPY it.
Cost of carrying an unused record = ~0. Cost of dropping a used mail record = SILENT
deliverability loss. The asymmetry decides every ambiguous case in favor of copying.

Operative plan without Chris:
  - COPY verbatim: all 12 M365 records + all 3 third-party senders (SES/Brevo/Resend)
    + both verification TXTs + the single working _dmarc (p=none).
  - DO NOT TOUCH mail auth: root SPF stays "...outlook.com -all" (no new includes);
    DMARC stays a single p=none (do NOT raise to quarantine/reject).
  - REPLACE only: root A/AAAA + www -> CloudFront, on our schedule (after cert+alias ready).
  - CARRY-THROUGH the VPS subdomains (dev, time, dev.time) at their current Linode IPs so
    the flip changes nothing for them; they retire naturally at VPS shutdown (a later,
    separate, IRREVERSIBLE step). Prune then, with eyes open.
  - DO NOT CREATE: NS, SOA (Route53 makes its own), _domainconnect (GoDaddy-only), and the
    two double-suffix-broken records (already dead — recreating them is pointless or harmful).
  - DROP ftp + autodiscover.admin: they CNAME to root, so a literal copy would wrongly follow
    root to CloudFront; both are VPS/legacy with no known M365 role. Reversible 2-min re-add.
Why this is safe without Chris: the DNS flip is REVERSIBLE (flip NS back to GoDaddy). The VPS
shutdown is NOT. So Chris's absence argues for a LONGER soak before shutdown, not for delaying
the migration. Migrate now; defer only the irreversible decommission.
